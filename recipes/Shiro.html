Ingredients

  • ½cup chickpea flour
  • 6green cardamom pods
  • 1large red onion, coarsely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons berbere (see note)
  • 3medium vine-ripened tomatoes (about 11 ounces), coarsely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the spice mix: Place cinnamon, coriander, fenugreek, peppercorns, cardamom and allspice in a small heavy skillet set over medium heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the smell is very aromatic and spices are lightly toasted, 2 to 3 minutes. Let cool.

  2. Prepare the shiro: Place the onion and garlic in a food processor, and pulse until very finely minced, stopping periodically to scrape down the sides of the bowl with a rubber spatula to ensure even chopping. Set aside.

  3. Increase heat to medium-high to bring shiro to a boil, then reduce heat to low, and simmer for about 5 minutes to cook off the raw taste of the chickpea flour and integrate all the flavors. Stir in the jalapeños and season to taste with salt.
